Verification Timeline

The verification process typically takes 5-7 business days. You'll receive status updates via email at each stage. Expedited verification (48 hours) is available for urgent campaigns with an additional fee.

Defining Milestones & Goals

Milestones are the foundation of transparency on MilestoneGive. Each milestone should represent a concrete, verifiable achievement with clear deliverables.

Milestone Best Practices:
✓ GOOD: Specific & Measurable

"Install 5 solar water pumps in designated villages, each with 500L daily capacity, documented with GPS coordinates and flow rate measurements."

✗ BAD: Vague & Unmeasurable

"Improve water access in the region."

Required Milestone Components:
{ "milestone_id": "M001", "title": "Site Preparation & Equipment Acquisition", "description": "Detailed description of what will be accomplished", "budget_allocation": 15000, // USD equivalent "timeline": { "start_date": "2024-01-15", "end_date": "2024-02-15", "duration_days": 31 }, "deliverables": [ "Land survey and soil analysis report", "Building permits from local authorities", "Equipment purchase receipts and photos", "Safety compliance certification" ], "verification_criteria": { "photo_evidence": true, "video_walkthrough": true, "third_party_audit": false, "receipts_required": true, "gps_location": true }, "payment_trigger": "auto_release_on_verification" }

Milestone Verification System

Our rigorous verification process ensures that donations are used as intended and milestones are genuinely achieved. This builds trust and accountability into every transaction.

Three-Tier Verification Model

MilestoneGive employs a multi-layered approach combining automated checks, community review, and professional auditing to ensure maximum transparency and accountability.

Tier 1: Automated

Smart contract validation, GPS verification, timestamp checking, document format validation

Tier 2: Community

Donor voting system, public comment period, reputation scoring, fraud reporting mechanisms

Tier 3: Professional

Third-party auditor review, on-site inspections (for large campaigns), financial statement analysis

Failure to Verify - Consequences

If a milestone cannot be verified within the specified timeframe, the following actions are triggered automatically:

  • • Funds for that milestone are frozen
  • • Donors are notified and can request refunds
  • • Campaign is flagged for review
  • • Creator must submit explanation and remediation plan
  • • Repeated failures result in platform suspension

Blockchain & Smart Contract Architecture

MilestoneGive is built on the Aptos blockchain, leveraging Move smart contracts for unprecedented security, transparency, and efficiency in charitable giving.

Why Aptos?

  • Speed: Sub-second transaction finality ensures instant donation confirmations
  • Low Cost: Transaction fees average $0.01-$0.05, keeping more money for causes
  • Security: Move language prevents common smart contract vulnerabilities
  • Scalability: 160,000+ TPS capacity handles massive donation volumes

Smart Contract Features

  • Escrow System: Funds locked until milestones verified
  • Auto-Release: Verified milestones trigger automatic fund distribution
  • Refund Mechanism: Donors can reclaim unspent funds if campaign fails
  • Immutable Records: All transactions permanently recorded on-chain

Understanding Gas Fees

Donation Transaction

~$0.01

Standard APT transfer to campaign contract

Milestone Verification

~$0.03

Update contract state with verification data

~$0.02

Gas Fee Coverage

MilestoneGive covers all gas fees for milestone verification and fund release transactions. Donors only pay the minimal gas fee for their donation transaction (~$0.01), ensuring maximum impact of every contribution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

How do I know my donation is being used properly?

Every donation on MilestoneGive is secured in a smart contract escrow. Funds are only released when specific milestones are verified through our multi-tier verification system, which includes photo evidence, community review, and third-party audits for larger campaigns. You can track exactly how your donation is being used in real-time through your donor dashboard.

What happens if a campaign doesn't reach its goal?

MilestoneGive uses a flexible funding model. Campaigns can proceed with partial funding if they have enough to complete at least one milestone. If a campaign fails to achieve any milestones or is cancelled, donors can claim refunds through the smart contract. Unspent funds are always returnable to donors.

Can I get a tax deduction for my cryptocurrency donation?

Tax treatment of cryptocurrency donations varies by jurisdiction. In many countries, donating appreciated cryptocurrency can provide tax advantages. We provide detailed transaction records and work with verified 501(c)(3) organizations (US) and equivalent registered charities globally. Consult with a tax professional in your area for specific guidance. Our platform automatically generates donation receipts with all necessary information for tax reporting.

How long does the verification process take?

Verification timelines vary based on campaign type and complexity:

  • • Small milestones (<$5,000): 3-7 days
  • • Medium milestones ($5,000-$25,000): 7-14 days
  • • Large milestones (>$25,000): 14-21 days (includes professional audit)

Automated checks happen within 24 hours, but community review and professional audits add time to ensure thoroughness.

What are the platform fees?

MilestoneGive operates on a transparent fee structure:

  • • Platform Fee: 5% of successfully distributed funds
  • • Payment Processing: Covered by platform (no additional charge to donors)
  • • Gas Fees: ~$0.01 per donation (paid by donor), all other blockchain fees covered by platform
  • • Verification Costs: Included in platform fee

This means 95% of every successfully distributed donation goes directly to the campaign. Fees are only charged when funds are released to campaign creators, not when donations are made.

Is my personal information kept private?

Yes. While donations are publicly visible on the blockchain (showing wallet addresses and amounts), your personal identity is not connected to your wallet address unless you choose to share it. Campaign creators and other donors only see your wallet address, not your name or personal details. You have full control over your privacy settings and can choose to donate anonymously or publicly display your support. We comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations globally.

Can I cancel a donation after it's been made?

Once a donation transaction is confirmed on the blockchain, it cannot be reversed. However, you have recourse in specific situations:

  • • Before Milestone Completion: If funds haven't been released to the campaign creator, you can request a refund if the campaign is cancelled or fails verification
  • • Fraud Detection: If a campaign is found to be fraudulent, all donors are automatically eligible for refunds
  • • Campaign Cancellation: If a creator cancels their campaign, all unspent funds are returned to donors
  • • Goal Not Met: For all-or-nothing campaigns that don't reach their target, full refunds are available

This is why our milestone-based system is so important - it protects your donation until verified progress is demonstrated.

What cryptocurrencies can I use to donate?

Currently, MilestoneGive accepts:

  • • APT (Aptos): Primary currency, lowest fees
  • • USDC on Aptos: Stablecoin option for price stability
  • • USDT on Aptos: Alternative stablecoin

We're built on Aptos blockchain to ensure fast, low-cost transactions. We plan to add cross-chain bridging capabilities in the future to accept donations from other major blockchains. All donations are automatically converted to the campaign creator's preferred currency at the time of milestone fund release.

Security Best Practices

Protect yourself and your donations by following these essential security guidelines.

Never Share Private Keys

MilestoneGive will NEVER ask for your wallet's private key or seed phrase. Anyone requesting this information is attempting fraud.

Verify URLs Carefully

Always check you're on the official MilestoneGive domain. Bookmark our site and avoid clicking links in unsolicited messages.

Enable 2FA

Activate two-factor authentication on your wallet and email account for an extra layer of security.

Review Transactions

Double-check recipient addresses and amounts before confirming any transaction in your wallet.

Report Suspicious Activity

If you notice anything unusual about a campaign or communication, report it immediately to our security team.

Secure Your Devices

Keep your devices updated, use strong passwords, and avoid accessing your wallet on public Wi-Fi.

Common Scam Alert

Be wary of campaigns promising unrealistic results, requesting donations outside the platform, or creating urgency through fear tactics. Legitimate campaigns are patient and transparent.
